# Part 3 — Reference Library

Source: [Email Marketing Architecture](https://app.notion.com/p/3a497ad572998101bd68c6d12bdd70c8) (Notion)

Five senders, chosen for fit with MWA's brand (character-led, gamified, wellness-adjacent) and this sequence's streak/habit goal. For each: what to **adopt**, what to **reject**. Statistics are third-party and sourced; treat them as directional, not MWA facts.

| Reference | Adopt | Reject (collides with MWA rules) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Duolingo** | Mascot as a recurring character (maps to Blue); celebrate real progress; short, visual, emotional; humane streak freeze/repair; identity framing ("you are a learner"). ~55% monthly DAU retention reported (Propel). | Guilt and FOMO subject lines, "quitter" shaming, loss-aversion pressure. |
| **Superhuman** | Selective, white-glove, personal tone; "you're the right fit" belonging; one quick win before any ask. | Manufactured-scarcity waitlist and hype-as-FOMO. |
| **Headspace** | Intrinsic motivation (tie to why they joined); set expectations for the whole sequence; action-triggered celebration ("1 for 1"); warm, un-stuffy tone. ~67% of opens on mobile (CleverTap). | Any mental-health efficacy or outcome claim without evidence. |
| **Morning Brew** | Reliability as habit (same rhythm builds an unbreakable routine); distinctive voice beats design; skimmable and mobile-first; A/B test subjects; list hygiene for deliverability. 40%+ open rates; deliverability ~60%→99% (Campaign Monitor). | Nothing to reject — but voice must be MWA's, not "millennial-cheeky." |
| **Strava** | Weekly (not daily) streak with buffer; challenge-completion unlocks a *genuine* reward/access; social belonging alongside the goal; cut volume, raise relevance (~30-40% of prior volume). | "Engage enough or lose your status" pressure emails — users call these "icky." No coercion, no comparison-as-pressure. |

> **Evidence note.** Every figure above is a third-party report about another company, not a measured MWA result. Use them to justify a tactic, never as a claim in our own copy.
