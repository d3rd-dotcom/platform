## The collision you need to know about first

LinkedIn's native "professional" register — the default tone most people write in on the platform — is explicitly what MWA's own style guide forbids. The repo's editorial anti-patterns reference literally calls this category "LinkedIn words" and lists them as banned: *optimize your life, unlock your potential, level up your life, your best self, transform your mindset, holistic, synergy, empower, journey of self-discovery, step into your best era*.

If you write a LinkedIn post the way LinkedIn posts are usually written, you will violate MWA's brand voice on the first sentence.

## What MWA sounds like

- Academic, visionary, and intellectually curious — real substance, no filler.
- Internet-aware, never trend-hopping.
- Calmly skeptical of AI and its hype — evidence first.
- Awe and intellectual rebellion, delivered calmly.
- Positive, encouraging, and clear — the lift comes from concrete wins, not hype adjectives.
- Statistics, theory, and academic framing.
- Cyberpunk philosophy, kept legible.

## What MWA does NOT sound like

| Register | Example | What to do instead |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Corporate wellness | "Optimize your life!" | Name the actual mechanism or the concrete win |
| Vague self-help | "Step into your best era" | Replace with the specific behavioral frame |
| Recruiter / referral-bait | "Invite your friends and earn!" | Cut it — talk about the work, not the funnel |
| Crypto-bro slang | WAGMI, ser, gm, ngmi, frens, anon, based, alpha, degen, ape in, diamond hands, to the moon | Zero of these ever, in any post |
| "This is X, not Y" framing | "This isn't another meditation app — it's a research lab." | State what it is: "A research lab for personal development, with Blue as your co-investigator." |

## The one test — run before posting anything

> Does this communicate an educational gameworld, an academic angle, or a feature that actually matters?

## Ground Then Elevate — the central rule

Every poetic or narrative claim must be preceded or followed by its concrete meaning. Concrete fact first, then narrative framing — never the reverse.

Bad (ungrounded): "A new awakening engine guides the self."

Good (grounded then elevated): "Blue is an AI agent with memory. She reviews platform data and submissions, and pays rewards herself. Her characterization makes her feel more like infrastructure than a feature."

### Hierarchy of Information

1. **What it is** — functional definition
2. **What it does** — practical reality
3. **Why it matters** — value proposition
4. **What it means** — narrative / philosophical layer

Most first drafts jump straight to #4. Reorder before publishing.

## What we are

**Mental Wealth Academy is a social-first gameworld for learning, research, and rewards.** A gameworld built around behavioral psychology, custom science tools, and Blue, our AI companion — with permanent rewards, real ownership, and anonymous-by-default participation.

## Who we're writing for

| Audience | Who | What they want |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Primary | Gen-Z individuals seeking structured growth, ages 18 and up | Grew up online; drawn to psychology and self-improvement; motivated by XP, rewards, and badges; want accountability, real stakes, a communal social environment; thrive on quick, well-scoped tasks |
| Secondary | Scientists and academics, including citizen scientists, status seekers, and special-interest groups | Collaborative testing and experimentation; research tied to live participants and real behavioral data; an AI companion that generates novel datasets |

LinkedIn's audience skews more professional and slightly older than MWA's Primary reader elsewhere. Default to Primary voice and substance unless a post is deliberately aimed at the Secondary academic/research audience. Never mix both audiences inside one post.

## Technology Stays Backstage (hard rule, zero exceptions on LinkedIn)

Never use, in any LinkedIn post, comment, or image text: **Base, $BLUE, blockchain, onchain, crypto, token, wallet, mint, burn, gas, NFT, DEX, smart contract, transaction hash**, or any ticker (including USDC — say "cash" or "a dollar bounty"). Acceptable reward nouns: "credits" and "Diamonds" (never with a ticker symbol or "$" prefix).

## Sentence-level mechanics

- Cut "that" constructions.
- Replace abstractions with specifics.
- Delete hedge words on sight: kind of, sort of, maybe, possibly, somewhat, a bit, fairly, pretty, just, simply, basically, essentially, really, very, quite.
- One idea per sentence.
- Active voice, present tense.
- Never set words or headings in all caps.
- Never bake a specific number, date, or stat into a reusable template or skeleton.

## Before You Publish

- Can someone explain what we do after reading this post?
- Is the point of the message certain and confident?
- Does every sentence deliver one clear thought or emotion?
- Have I named tangible benefits the reader can picture?
- Is every poetic phrase grounded in something concrete?
- Does the order go real first, story second?
- Is there only one main idea per paragraph?
- Have I cut sentences that sound cool but mean nothing?
- Did I avoid "this is X, not Y" phrasing?
- Would an investor understand this? Would an artist feel it?

This checklist runs alongside — not instead of — the mandatory humanization pass.
