# compliance-guardrails.md — anti-manipulation rules

Source: [LinkedIn Content Architecture](https://app.notion.com/p/3a297ad57299817ab638fdd08d68d565) (Notion)

MWA's brand position is against using persuasion tactics on its own audience — the product's own case studies critique this kind of pressure.

## Rejected tactics

- **Fake scarcity and resetting countdowns.** Any scarcity/time-limit claim must be real, with its exact reason and limit stated.
- **Investment-coded urgency.** No price talk, profit implication, "early" status, future-value hints, or reward-as-investment framing.
- **Undisclosed creator or participant relationships.** Any material connection must be disclosed with the endorsement itself, repeated for recurring content.
- **Unsubstantiated mental-health outcomes.** No diagnosis, treatment, prevention, or efficacy claims without evidence.
- **Manufactured consensus.** No bought engagement, fake accounts, cherry-picked totals — state unit, window, source, limitation for every metric.
- **Reward-first acquisition.** No vague giveaways or tasks whose only purpose is claiming a reward.
- **Parasocial pressure from Blue.** She should never imply a member owes her attendance, spending, disclosure, or constant engagement.
- **Minor-directed financial promotion.** MWA's primary audience begins at 18 — keep targeting and copy consistent with that.
