# post-skeletons.md — PAS / AIDA / BAB

Source: [LinkedIn Content Architecture](https://app.notion.com/p/3a297ad57299817ab638fdd08d68d565) (Notion)

Evergreen copywriting skeletons — a starting shape, not a formula. Every post still goes through the full workflow, including the mandatory humanization pass.

## PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solve

1. **Problem** — name a real problem the reader recognizes.
2. **Agitate** — make the real cost concrete and specific (not manufactured urgency).
3. **Solve** — the concrete MWA answer, following Ground Then Elevate.

## AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

1. **Attention** — a hook under the character limit.
2. **Interest** — a specific, concrete detail.
3. **Desire** — connect to something the reader actually wants, grounded in a real feature.
4. **Action** — what to do next. No external link in the caption — first comment only.

## BAB — Before, After, Bridge

1. **Before** — the state of things before a change.
2. **After** — the state after — every claim needs the evidence-discipline check.
3. **Bridge** — the specific mechanism connecting them. The part most likely to get cut when rushing — don't skip it.

None of these skeletons override Ground Then Elevate or skip the mandatory humanization pass.
