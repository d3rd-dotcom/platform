# content-pillars.md — candidate topic buckets

Source: [LinkedIn Content Architecture](https://app.notion.com/p/3a297ad57299817ab638fdd08d68d565) (Notion)

Candidate topic categories, not a mandatory rotation. Every post from a pillar still needs the full voice/evidence/compliance/format/humanization process.

1. **Curiosity sets the syllabus.** One question, one guide, one next step.
2. **Blue teaches the interface.** Short, concrete explanations of a real product moment.
3. **Field notes make learning accountable.** Prompt, safe excerpt, next step — apply the mental-health guardrail carefully here.
4. **Knowledge compounds socially.** The guide-map, prerequisite-chain idea — a Knowledge DAG named-framework candidate.
5. **Start with one mission.** Small, well-scoped actions with a clear completion state.
6. **Education and structural access.** Sourced explainers about literacy, funding, access — any statistic here must clear the evidence-discipline check in full.
7. **Research in public.** One validated assessment/case study/method/limitation at a time — strong "worth bookmarking and commenting on" format.
8. **Rewards with conditions.** Review gates and real member value, no infrastructure vocabulary.
9. **The living library.** Guide prerequisites, levels, the map — why one canonical guide beats duplicate tutorials.
