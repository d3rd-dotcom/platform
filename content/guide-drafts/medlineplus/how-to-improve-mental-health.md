Mental health includes your emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It
shapes how you think, feel, and act, how you handle stress, relate to others, and
make choices. It matters at every stage of life.

**Why it matters**

Good mental health can help you cope with stress, stay physically healthy, keep
good relationships, contribute to your community, work productively, and reach
your potential.

**Ways to take care of it**

- Stay positive. This doesn't mean never feeling sadness or anger; it means
  keeping a balance, holding on to good feelings, and limiting time with negative
  information like an upsetting news or social media cycle.
- Practice gratitude. Each day, think about or write down what you're thankful
  for, big or small.
- Take care of your physical health. Your body and mind are connected, so stay
  active, get enough quality sleep, and eat a well-balanced diet.
- Connect with others. Strong relationships help protect against stress. Reach for
  a variety of connections, including community groups or a shared hobby.
- Develop a sense of meaning and purpose, through work, volunteering, learning, or
  spirituality.
- Build coping skills for handling stressful situations so you can stay flexible
  and keep at a hard problem.

**Calming practices to try**

- Meditation: focus your attention in a quiet spot, in a comfortable position,
  letting thoughts come and go without judging them.
- Relaxation techniques that produce the body's natural relaxation response, which
  slows breathing, lowers blood pressure, and eases muscle tension. These include
  progressive muscle relaxation, guided imagery, biofeedback, and self-hypnosis.
- Deep breathing: slow, deep, even breaths to calm body and mind.

It's also important to recognize when you need to get help. Start by talking with
a health care provider for advice on where to get care.
