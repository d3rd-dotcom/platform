topic: Anxiety
---

Anxiety is a feeling of fear, dread, and unease. It might make you sweat, feel
restless and tense, and have a rapid heartbeat. It can be a normal reaction to
stress, and in small doses it can even help you focus or give you a boost of
energy before something important. It becomes a bigger problem when the fear
doesn't go away and starts to feel overwhelming.

**Common forms it takes**

- Generalized: excessive, near-daily worry about ordinary things like health,
  money, work, and family.
- Panic: sudden, repeated periods of intense fear when there's no real danger,
  coming on quickly and lasting minutes or more.
- Phobias: intense fear of something that poses little or no actual danger, such
  as flying, crowded places, or social situations.

**What can raise the risk**

- Certain personality traits, like being shy or withdrawn in new situations
- Traumatic events in childhood or adulthood
- A family history of anxiety
- Some physical health conditions, such as thyroid problems

**Common symptoms**

- Anxious thoughts that are hard to control and interfere with daily life
- Physical signs like a pounding heart, unexplained aches, dizziness, or
  shortness of breath
- Avoiding everyday activities you used to do

Caffeine, other substances, and certain medicines can make symptoms worse.

**What can help**

Talk therapy can help you recognize and shift troubling thoughts and behaviors.
Cognitive behavioral therapy teaches different ways of thinking and reacting to
the things that trigger fear. Acceptance and commitment therapy uses strategies
like mindfulness and goal-setting. Support groups and stress-management
techniques may also help.
