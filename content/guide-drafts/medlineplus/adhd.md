ADHD centers on three things: trouble paying attention, trouble controlling
impulsive behavior, and being overly active. It usually shows up in
childhood and often lasts into adulthood, though some people don't notice
it until they're adults.

It's normal to sometimes have trouble focusing or sitting still. With ADHD the
symptoms are more intense, ongoing, and show up across settings like home,
school, and work.

**What it can look like**

Attention-related: missing details, trouble staying focused, not seeming to
listen, losing things, forgetting daily tasks, avoiding work that takes sustained
mental effort.

Hyperactive or impulsive: fidgeting, restlessness, trouble with quiet activities,
talking a lot, blurting out answers, trouble waiting a turn, interrupting.

**What shapes it**

The exact cause isn't known. It likely comes from a mix of genetics and
environment. Researchers are looking at factors like brain injuries, nutrition,
and social environments.

**What can help**

There's no cure, but several approaches can reduce symptoms and improve day-to-day
functioning, including talk therapy and behavior-focused strategies. A healthy
lifestyle with good sleep, regular exercise, and steady eating can help too. For
students, school-based support like organizational skills or extra time on tests
can make a difference.
