Most people feel sad or irritable from time to time and say they're in a bad
mood. A mood disorder is different: it affects a person's everyday emotional
state rather than passing quickly.

**What to know**

Nearly one in ten people aged 18 and older experience a mood disorder. Beyond the
emotional toll, it can raise the risk for other health problems like heart
disease and diabetes.

**What can help**

Support ranges from talk therapy to other approaches a provider can walk you
through. With support, most people with a mood disorder can lead productive
lives.
