topic: Stress

Everyone experiences stress, your body's physical and emotional response to new
or challenging situations. It can show up around work, school, health, and
relationships. A normal amount can help build your sense that you can solve
problems, but when stress runs long term it can wear on your health.

**How stress can show up**

- Feelings of fear, anger, sadness, worry, numbness, or frustration
- Changes in appetite, energy, desires, and interests
- Trouble concentrating and making decisions
- Nightmares or problems sleeping
- Physical reactions like headaches, body pains, stomach problems, or skin rashes
- Increased use of alcohol, drugs, and other substances

**Take care of your mind**

- Take breaks from news and social media; constant negative information can wear
  on you
- Make time to unwind, take deep breaths, stretch, or meditate
- Keep a journal
- Spend time outdoors, active or relaxing
- Do other relaxing activities you enjoy
- Practice gratitude daily; write down specific things you're grateful for
- Connect with people you trust, and with community or faith-based groups

**Take care of your body**

- Get enough sleep. Go to bed and wake up at the same time each day. Adults need
  7 or more hours a night.
- Move more. Start small and build toward about 2.5 hours a week, broken into
  chunks like 20 to 30 minutes a day.
- Eat healthy: fruits and vegetables, lean protein, whole grains, and low-fat
  dairy. Limit unhealthy fats, salt, and added sugars.
- Limit alcohol
- Avoid misusing drugs, and don't take someone else's prescription
- Avoid smoking, vaping, and other tobacco products
- Keep up with regular health appointments, screenings, and vaccinations

Everyone manages stress differently. Take small steps to find the combination of
healthy techniques that work for you.
