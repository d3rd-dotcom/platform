Social connection is the size and variety of your relationships, the roles they
play, and their quality. Feeling connected means having the number, quality, and
variety of relationships you want, and a sense that you belong and have the
support you need. Supportive connections are tied to longer, healthier lives.

**Why it matters**

High-quality relationships can help people live longer and lower the risk of
serious health problems like heart disease, stroke, and low mood or anxiety.
Connection can also improve your ability to manage stress, support healthy eating
and activity, and improve sleep quality.

**How it works**

People are social by nature, and relationships with family, friends, coworkers,
and community members matter. Stable, supportive relationships give you what you
need to cope with hard stretches, and people with healthy relationships are more
likely to make healthy choices. Connections offer:

- Emotional support: people you can turn to in good times and hard times
- Physical support: a ride to an appointment, or help with childcare on short
  notice

**Community connections**

Communities that offer supportive connections matter too. Safe public places like
neighborhoods, schools, places of worship, workplaces, and parks encourage people
to connect, reduce isolation and loneliness, and give community members a way to
give back, which strengthens the bonds further.
