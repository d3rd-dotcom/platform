Physical activity is one of the most useful things you can do for your body and
mind, and some of the benefits show up right away.

**Immediate brain benefits**

Some brain benefits happen right after a session of moderate-to-vigorous
activity, including sharper thinking and reduced short-term feelings of anxiety.
Over time, regular activity keeps thinking, learning, and judgment skills sharp,
lowers the risk of low mood and anxiety, and helps you sleep better.

**Managing your weight**

Both your eating patterns and activity routines matter for weight. To maintain
weight, work up to about 150 minutes a week of moderate-intensity activity, such
as 30 minutes a day for five days, or whatever fits your life. People vary a lot
in how much they need.

**Reducing health risks**

Regular activity can lower your risk of heart disease and stroke, help lower blood
pressure and improve cholesterol, and reduce the risk of type 2 diabetes. It may
also help reduce the risk of serious outcomes from infectious diseases, and it's
tied to lower risk for several common cancers.

**Bones, muscles, and daily function**

As you age, protecting your bones, joints, and muscles helps you keep doing daily
activities. Muscle-strengthening activities like lifting weights help you build or
maintain strength, and a mix of aerobic, strengthening, and balance activities
lowers the risk of falls for older adults.

**Living longer**

More activity is tied to a lower risk of early death. Even 10 minutes more a day
makes a difference, and taking more steps each day helps too. Regular activity can
also help people manage existing chronic conditions, reducing pain and improving
mood and quality of life.

**How much you need**

Recommendations vary by age and situation. For most people, moderate activity like
brisk walking is generally safe, so you can start where you are and build up.
