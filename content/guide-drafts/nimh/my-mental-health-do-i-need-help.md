It helps to take a moment to notice how you've been feeling and how it's
affecting your daily life.

**Signs you might be running low**

- Feeling sad, stressed, worried, tearful, or hopeless
- Feeling irritable or frustrated
- Feeling tired or low on energy
- Feeling less interested in things you usually enjoy
- Trouble sleeping, or changes in appetite or weight
- Difficulty concentrating or making simple decisions
- Trouble completing your usual tasks and activities

**Things that may help you feel better**

- Exercise regularly, eat well, and aim for 7-9 hours of sleep
- Spend time with loved ones
- Practice meditation or breathing exercises
- Make time for low-stress activities you enjoy
- Volunteer to help others

If these don't help, or things feel like they're getting worse, it's worth
reaching out to a health care provider. There are ways to feel better, and
they can help you find the next steps.

**If you're having thoughts of suicide, get help now**

Contact a local crisis line or emergency service right away.
