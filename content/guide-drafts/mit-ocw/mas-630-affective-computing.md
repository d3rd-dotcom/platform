This is MIT's MAS.630 Affective Computing (Fall 2015), a graduate, project-driven course on building technologies that measure and communicate emotion, read emotion respectfully, and respond to it intelligently — systems designed with emotional-intelligence mechanisms inspired by how emotion works naturally.

**Course basics**

One 3-hour lecture session per week. MIT students need instructor permission to enroll. Students are expected to attend all classes and all project/proposal presentations.

**Sample topics**

Final topics are chosen with class input; the instructor's suggested areas include emotionally intelligent human-computer interaction; emotion and perception, decision-making, and creativity; emotion and learning; the physiology of emotion; neuroscience findings related to emotion; affect recognition by machines, including wearable systems; communicating frustration and stress in autism and customer experience contexts; responding to user emotion to reduce frustration; robots and agents that carry emotion; expression of emotion by machines and synthetic characters; the philosophical, social, and ethical implications of affective computing; machine and mobile empathy and emotional support; and lie detection and stress detection.

**Grading**

Classroom participation: 20%. Weekly reading/response assignments: 35%. Final project and presentation: 45%.
