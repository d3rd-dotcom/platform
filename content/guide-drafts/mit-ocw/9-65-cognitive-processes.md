This is MIT's 9.65 Cognitive Processes (Spring 2004, taught by Prof. Mary C. Potter), a course in human experimental cognitive psychology — the study of how people process information intelligently. It covers vision, attention, memory, learning, concepts, imagery, language, mental codes, reasoning, and problem-solving, with an emphasis on experimental methods and evidence.

**Course basics**

Prerequisite: 9.00 Introduction to Psychology. Required textbook: Reisberg, *Cognition: Exploring the Science of the Mind*, 2nd ed. Meets twice weekly for 1.5-hour sessions, plus three mandatory in-class labs analyzing cognitive processes in memory and vision.

**Grading**

Lab participation and reports: 24%. Two in-class quizzes: 48%. Final exam (2 hours): 28%.

**Note**

The syllabus stresses that attendance at labs and quizzes is essential — handouts alone don't cover everything needed for the course.
