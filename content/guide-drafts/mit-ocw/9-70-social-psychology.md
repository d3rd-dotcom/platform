This is MIT's 9.70 Social Psychology (Spring 2013), an undergraduate course on interpersonal and group dynamics — how individual thoughts, feelings, and actions are shaped by group beliefs, values, and practices. It runs through in-class activities, small study groups, homework, occasional lectures, and demonstrations rather than a standard lecture sequence.

**Course structure**

One 3-hour lecture session per week, with an expected 12 hours of work per week over 14 weeks. The course frames its material through two contrasting paradigms: the "modern" experimental paradigm represented in the textbook, and the "systemic/contextual/post-modern" paradigm the instructors favor.

**Core topics**

Social influence processes; collaborative learning systems; human inquiry and scientific methodology; meaning and power dynamics in social systems; generic versus unique patterns in human social organization.

**Grading**

There are no problem sets, quizzes, or a final exam. Grades come from a formative and summative evaluation process based on attendance and participation quality, study group minutes, facilitation responsibilities, timesheet documentation, journal maintenance, and term project participation — evaluated at the individual, group, and class level.
