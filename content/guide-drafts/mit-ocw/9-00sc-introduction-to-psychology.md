This is MIT's 9.00SC Introduction to Psychology (Fall 2011, taught by Prof. John D. E. Gabrieli), a survey of the scientific study of human nature — how the mind works and how the brain supports it. It covers the mental and neural foundations of perception, emotion, learning, memory, cognition, development, personality, and social interaction, and takes on bigger questions like nature versus nurture, free will, consciousness, and the self.

**Topics covered, in order**

Brain structure and research methods; vision and visual perception; consciousness and attention; memory systems and amnesia; learning processes; language and cognitive abilities; thinking and intelligence; emotion and motivation; personality psychology; child and adult development; stress and coping; psychopathology; social psychology; evolutionary psychology and happiness.

**Course format**

Lecture videos, reading assignments (including a free online textbook), self-check questions, discussion materials, and three non-cumulative exams (multiple-choice and short-answer). Enrolled MIT students also complete two writing assignments analyzing scientific readings on a controversial topic in psychology.

**Emotion and motivation unit**

The emotion and motivation lecture (Lecture 15) draws on Chapter 10, "Emotion and Motivations: Feeling and Striving," from Kosslyn & Rosenberg's *Psychology in Context*. It covers the components of emotion, how emotion gets expressed, and the brain regions involved in processing, regulating, and perceiving emotion. A companion discussion page covers emotion theories — James-Lange, Cannon-Bard, and cognitive theories — along with emotion's role in decision-making and learning.
