topic: Social Influence and Groups
---

Social influence is the way other people, group expectations, roles, and settings
shape what someone notices and does. Influence can help a group coordinate. It
can also make a person ignore evidence, hide uncertainty, or follow a harmful
request.

**Three routes of influence**

- Informational influence happens when people use others as a source of evidence
  in an uncertain situation.
- Normative influence happens when belonging or approval affects behavior.
- Authority influence happens when a role or institution carries power to direct
  action.

People can experience several routes at once. A classroom may have a rule, a
teacher's request, and a peer norm all pointing in the same direction.

**Design a healthier group decision**

Before discussion, let each person write an independent answer. During the
conversation, ask for evidence and invite a minority view. Name the decision
rule, such as a vote or a consensus attempt. After the decision, record what
would count as evidence that the plan needs revision. These steps protect the
group from confusing agreement with accuracy.

Influence is shaped by context. A person may act differently with friends, at
school, online, or in a formal role. Study the setting before judging the person.
Consent, safety, and the right to ask questions matter in every group.

Notice one group decision this week. What information was available? Who spoke
first? What pressures were visible? What would make the next conversation more
open and more evidence-based?
