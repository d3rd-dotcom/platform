## MAS.630 Affective Computing (Fall 2015)

### Course Description

The course teaches students to develop technologies that measure and communicate emotion, read emotion respectfully, and respond intelligently to it. Students learn to create systems with emotional intelligence mechanisms inspired by how emotions function naturally.

### Meeting Times

Lectures: 1 session/week, 3 hours/session

### Sample Topics

The instructor noted these potential areas of study (final topics selected with class input):

- Emotionally Intelligent Human Computer Interaction
- Emotion and Perception, Decision-making, and Creativity
- Emotion and Learning
- Physiology of Emotion
- Neuroscience Findings Related to Emotion
- Affect Recognition by Machines (including wearable systems)
- Communicating Frustration/Stress in Autism and Customer Experience
- Responding to User Emotion to Reduce Frustration
- Robots/Agents that "have" Emotion
- Expression of Emotion by Machines/Agents/Synthetic characters
- Philosophical, Social, Ethical Implications of Affective Computing
- Machine/Mobile Empathy and Emotional Support
- Lie Detection and Stress Detection
