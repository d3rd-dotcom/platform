## 9.70 Social Psychology (Spring 2013)

### Course Description

This undergraduate course examines interpersonal and group dynamics, investigating how individual thoughts, feelings, and actions are influenced by group beliefs, values, and practices. Learning occurs through in-class activities, small study groups, homework assignments, occasional lectures, and demonstrations.

### Course Meeting Times

- Lectures: 1 session/week; 3 hours/session
- Expected commitment: 12 hours/week for 14 weeks

### Primary Topics and Themes

The syllabus emphasizes several interconnected areas, framed through two contrasting scientific paradigms:

- The "modern" experimental paradigm (represented in the textbook)
- The "systemic/contextual/post-modern" paradigm (advocated by instructors)

Core conceptual areas:

- Social influence processes
- Collaborative learning systems
- Human inquiry and science methodology
- Meaning and power dynamics in social systems
- Generic vs. unique patterns in human social organization

### Grading Structure

No traditional assessments. The syllabus states: "There are no problem sets to be turned in, no quizzes, no final exam in 9.70."

Final grades result from a "formative and summative evaluation process" rather than instructor-determined scores, depending on:

- Attendance and participation quality
- Study group minutes submission
- Facilitation responsibilities
- Timesheet documentation of effort
- Journal maintenance
- Term project participation
- Collaborative contribution at individual, group, and class levels

The document emphasizes that "successful on-task performance by all constituents" across all organizational levels determines final grades.
