## 9.65 Cognitive Processes (Spring 2004)

Instructor: Prof. Mary C. Potter, Department of Brain and Cognitive Sciences.

### Course Description

The course explores "human experimental cognitive psychology, the study of human intelligent processing." Topics include vision, attention, memory, learning, concepts, imagery, language, mental codes, reasoning, and problem-solving, with emphasis on experimental methods and evidence.

### Prerequisites

9.00 (Introduction to Psychology)

### Required Textbook

Reisberg, Daniel. *Cognition: Exploring the Science of The Mind*. 2nd ed. Norton, 2001.

### Course Structure

- Meetings: 2 sessions weekly, 1.5 hours each
- Labs: Three mandatory in-class labs analyzing cognitive processes in memory and vision

### Grading Breakdown

- Lab participation with reports: 24%
- Two in-class quizzes: 48%
- Final exam (2 hours): 28%
