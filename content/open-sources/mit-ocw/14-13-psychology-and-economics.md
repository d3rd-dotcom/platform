## 14.13 Psychology and Economics (Spring 2020)

Instructor: Prof. Frank Schilbach, Department of Economics.

### Course Description

"Psychology and Economics (aka Behavioral Economics) is a growing subfield of economics that incorporates insights from psychology and other social sciences into economics." The course examines how human behavior systematically deviates from mainstream economic assumptions, with applications spanning labor economics, public economics, industrial organization, health economics, finance, and development economics.

### Course Details

- Level: Undergraduate and Graduate
- Prerequisite: 14.01 Principles of Microeconomics or an equivalent introductory microeconomics course
- Meetings: 2 lectures/week (1.5 hours each); 1 recitation/week (1 hour)

### Grading Breakdown

- Exams (70%): Midterm (30%) and cumulative final (40%); final exam grade can replace midterm
- Problem Sets (25%): Five problem sets; lowest score automatically dropped
- In-Class Quizzes (5%): Brief unannounced quizzes testing knowledge of readings and lectures

### Notes

"Limited Attention" (Lecture 14) and "Happiness and Mental Health" (Lecture 22) are the two most directly relevant units for a Focus / Emotional Regulation prerequisite chain — both are strong ordering signals (this course places Limited Attention after establishing preference theory, and Happiness/Mental Health near the end after social-preference and behavioral-bias foundations are laid).
