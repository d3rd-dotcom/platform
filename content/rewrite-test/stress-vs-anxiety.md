## Rewrite (guide-body draft, ~150 words)

```markdown
Stress is a reaction to something happening now — a deadline, an argument,
a change you didn't choose. Anxiety is that same reaction without the
trigger going away, or without any trigger at all.

**Telling them apart**

- Stress usually has a specific cause: a test, an argument, a deadline.
- Stress goes away once the situation resolves.
- Anxiety can be constant, even without a current threat.

**Shared symptoms**

- Excessive worry
- Tension or restlessness
- Trouble sleeping
- Headaches or body pain

**Coping techniques**

- Keep a journal.
- Try breathing or mindfulness exercises.
- Exercise and eat regular meals.
- Keep a consistent sleep schedule.
- Limit caffeine.
- Reach out to people who help you cope.

**When to get more support**

If stress or anxiety doesn't go away, or it's stopping you from doing
things, consider talking to a professional. If you're in crisis, call or
text 988.
```
