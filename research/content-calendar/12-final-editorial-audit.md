# Final editorial, compliance, and execution audit

**Artifact reviewed:** `research/content-calendar/10-calendar-draft.json`  
**Governing standard:** `EDITORIAL.md` v4.0 and the `mwa-editorial` review rules  
**Evidence constraints:** `04-first-wave-fact-check.md`, `07-young-adult-media.md`, `08-blue-radio-positioning.md`, and `09-source-pack.md`  
**Review date:** 2026-07-17

## Overall verdict

**No-go as written. Go after the mandatory corrections below are applied and every evidence-dependent post clears its hold condition.**

The draft has a strong academic spine. It avoids emojis, overt referral bait, chain vocabulary in public copy, generic AI superlatives, fabricated audience scale, and mental-health treatment claims. Blue Radio is described accurately as a prerecorded, synchronized orientation broadcast rather than a live tutor. The remaining problems are fixable: one explicit contrast frame, one unsupported virtual-host outcome hook, one artificial urgency line, one privacy sentence that asks an off-screen demo to prove too much, several multi-action CTAs, one broken local source path, and incomplete hold language on dated evidence posts.

This is also a content strategy rather than a fully realistic solo-founder production schedule until one operating limit is added: **one master asset per day**. Comma-separated channels should be treated as distribution targets for the same asset. Every `repurpose` item should be backlog unless it can be cut from the master asset without a second shoot, new research, or a new design build.

## Verdict key

- **Go:** publishable after the normal day-of product and link check.
- **Revise:** exact replacement below is mandatory before scheduling.
- **Hold:** the copy may be sound, but publication requires named evidence, consent, deployed behavior, or production state. A fixed date never overrides the hold.

## Mandatory campaign-level corrections

1. **Apply every Day 1–30 replacement marked Revise.** Rows marked Hold may keep their copy only after the named evidence gate clears; use the provided fallback where one exists.
2. **Add a production cap:** one master asset per day. Cross-post the same asset where the format fits. Treat all `repurpose` work as backlog unless it requires no second shoot, new research pass, or new designed asset.
3. **Create a preflight ledger with one row per day:** source opened, product route tested, consent stored, disclosure added, metric instrumented, and hold cleared. Days 12, 14–16, 18–19, and 24–30 cannot be auto-scheduled.
4. **Do not let a calendar date defeat a hold.** Prepare an evergreen replacement post for every held day. Safe replacements are a current guide walkthrough, a sourced media-literacy lesson, or a founder-owned field note with no participant claim.
5. **Keep Blue Radio within the verified position:** prerecorded, synchronized, ten chapters, about 4:46, and an orientation broadcast. Do not call it live, interactive, personalized, an educator, a tutor, continuous as an uptime guarantee, 24 hours of unique programming, or better than a manual.
6. **Keep public copy free of infrastructure.** Internal `proof_or_source` fields may name routes, ledgers, and implementation files. Hooks, captions, on-screen labels, and CTAs should use guides, quests, credits, reviews, and reward records.
7. **Instrument before claiming.** The campaign’s primary event needs a defined analytics event and a working content-source parameter before Day 1. Day 15 and Day 30 must report only measures that were defined and collected consistently.
8. **Keep one CTA per post.** The measurable action should match the KPI. Secondary actions belong in the product after the click, not in the social caption.

## Final go/no-go

**No-go for automatic scheduling in its current form.**  
**Go for production after the mandatory revisions are applied, the one-asset-per-day limit is adopted, analytics are verified, and held posts are replaced or cleared individually.**

With those corrections, the calendar is specific, academic, product-connected, and defensible. It presents Blue as a distinctive host with real boundaries, turns research into useful actions, and avoids the hype mechanics the campaign is meant to criticize.
