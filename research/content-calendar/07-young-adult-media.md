# U.S. Young-Adult Media and Learning: Evidence Memo

**Research question:** What do people ages 18–25 actually use, watch, hear, trust, and learn from, and what should Mental Wealth Academy (MWA) do with Blue Radio?

**Prepared:** 2026-07-17  
**Scope:** United States wherever a U.S. source exists. No high-quality national dataset consistently isolates ages 18–25, so every finding below names the closest available band. “Gen Z” results are used only when the source discloses its birth-year definition.  
**Reading rule:** “Evidence” reports the source. “MWA implication” is an inference to test.

## Executive finding

The streaming hypothesis is **partly supported**. Young adults consume a great deal of streamed video and audio, but “streaming” is too broad to be a strategy. The strongest recent evidence favors creator-led social video for discovery, YouTube for reach and depth, and audio/podcasts for habitual or background use. It does not show that an always-on stream, by itself, will acquire or teach this audience effectively. [S1][S2][S3][S4]

Blue Radio should function as a **live educator and product guide with a 24-hour home**, then produce short clips, searchable explanations, course demonstrations, and audio recaps for the channels where young adults already discover content. The avatar can earn attention. The lesson, interaction, and demonstrated usefulness must earn trust.

## 1. Where young adults are reachable

Pew’s 2025 nationally representative U.S. survey reports the following self-reported platform reach among adults ages 18–29: YouTube 95%, Instagram 80%, Facebook 68%, TikTok 63%, Snapchat 58%, Reddit 48%, WhatsApp 37%, and X 33%. These figures describe whether a person **ever uses** a platform; they do not measure attention, intent, or conversion. The age band also includes people ages 26–29. [S1]

Pew separately found that roughly half of U.S. adults ages 18–29 used TikTok daily in early 2025, while a majority used YouTube daily. Its frequency survey covered 5,123 U.S. adults, recruited through a probability-based panel. Exact daily YouTube and TikTok percentages for the age band are displayed graphically rather than tabulated in the article, so this memo does not invent a point estimate. [S1]

### MWA implication

- Use **YouTube as the anchor channel**. It combines near-universal reach in the closest age band with long-form, Shorts, live, replay, search, and podcast behavior.
- Use **Instagram and TikTok as discovery channels**, with each post leading to one concrete lesson, tool, course, or Blue Radio segment.
- Treat **Reddit as a research and discussion environment**, not a broadcast feed. The audience is smaller than YouTube, Instagram, or TikTok and context matters.
- Do not allocate the calendar by platform reach alone. Run channel-specific tests for qualified visits, course starts, guide completions, returning listeners, and saves.

## 2. Streaming is real, but social video competes with television-style streaming

Deloitte’s 2025 Digital Media Trends survey found that 56% of its Gen Z respondents said social media content was more relevant to them than television shows and movies. This cohort spent 54% more time on social platforms and user-generated content than the average respondent, and 26% less time watching television and movies. Roughly half of its Gen Z respondents said they preferred videos on social media to videos on streaming-video services. The study was an online survey of 3,595 U.S. consumers in October 2024, weighted to Census benchmarks. Deloitte defined Gen Z as people born from 1997–2010, which included minors and adults older than 25 at fielding. [S2]

This supports a creator-led video strategy. It does not prove that every young adult prefers short video, or that polished long-form programming has no role. Deloitte’s average respondent reported a total of about six daily hours across media and entertainment, with individual mixes spanning streaming video, social media, games, music, podcasts, and other media. [S2]

### MWA implication

- Build each Blue Radio idea as a **content system**, not a single broadcast:
  1. a 20–45 second question or surprising finding;
  2. a 2–5 minute explanation or product demonstration;
  3. a live or long-form session;
  4. a written source card and direct route into the relevant guide, course, quest, or tool.
- Open with the intellectual problem. Show Blue navigating the product within the first few minutes of a longer segment.
- Make the stream replayable by topic. A 24-hour schedule without chapters, timestamps, titles, and clips sacrifices most of the discovery value.

## 3. Audio and podcasts support a persistent “radio” layer

Edison Research’s Share of Ear reported that U.S. people ages 13–34 spent four hours and 30 minutes per day with audio in Q1 2026. The measure includes AM/FM and its streams, streaming music, music videos on YouTube, audio and video podcasts, satellite radio, owned music, audiobooks, and television music channels. The band includes minors and people ages 26–34, and Edison’s public article does not publish the subgroup sample size. [S3]

Within spoken-word audio, Edison reported that podcasts accounted for 53% of listening time among U.S. people ages 13–34, compared with 23% for AM/FM radio. This is a share of spoken-word time, not a percentage of people who listen. [S4]

Edison’s Infinite Dial 2026 found monthly online-audio reach of 81% and monthly podcast consumption of 58% among Americans ages 12 and older. These national figures confirm broad audio adoption but are not young-adult estimates. The January 2026 survey included 2,050 U.S. participants ages 12 and older. [S5]

### MWA implication

- Keep Blue Radio always available, while designing **appointment segments** people can anticipate: morning briefing, study block, course clinic, research room, and evening recap.
- Offer an audio-first mode for commuting, chores, gaming, and studying. Pair each spoken explanation with a visible source card and a short text summary.
- Publish selected sessions as a podcast or podcast-style video. Edison describes podcasts as a dual audio/video medium; YouTube was the most-used podcast service among U.S. weekly podcast listeners in its 2025 reporting. That service figure is not age-specific. [S6]
- Judge the radio layer on returning listeners, qualified listening time, live questions answered, and post-listen product actions. Raw broadcast hours are an output, not an audience result.

## 4. Gaming is a useful interaction grammar

Deloitte’s 2026 Digital Media Monitor reports that 90% of its Gen Z respondents identified as gamers, and Gen Z and millennial gamers averaged eight hours of play per week. Deloitte’s underlying 2026 study surveyed 3,575 U.S. consumers online in October and November 2025, weighted to Census benchmarks, and defined Gen Z as 1997–2011. That band includes minors and people older than 25. [S7]

The Entertainment Software Association’s 2026 industry study also reports broad weekly play among Gen Z, which it defined as ages 14–29. Because the ESA represents game companies and the public webpage renders the generation-level reach inside a chart, this memo uses Deloitte for the numerical estimate and treats the ESA as corroborating industry evidence. [S8]

### MWA implication

- Use game conventions already native to MWA: a clear mission, visible progress, a bounded challenge, feedback, and a meaningful completion state.
- Let Blue demonstrate one action at a time. “Choose a guide, complete this checkpoint, return with the result” is stronger than a tour of every feature.
- Do not turn every lesson into points and streaks. The calendar should alternate challenge posts with explanations, research, member work, and reflective prompts.

## 5. VTubers have material reach; usefulness and interaction matter more than the avatar alone

YouTube reported that a sample of 300 virtual creators generated more than 15 billion global views in 2024, including one billion U.S. views, across videos, livestreams, and Shorts. It also reported that 57% of surveyed online 14–44-year-olds had watched a VTuber or virtual influencer in the prior 12 months. The report is platform-authored, its 57% age band is far wider than 18–25, and its nearby footnotes describe different fan samples without a complete recruitment or weighting methodology. Treat the percentage as directional market evidence, not a population estimate. [S9]

A double-blind peer-reviewed study of 301 Gen Z VTuber viewers found that content, personality, avatar design, interaction, community, companionship, and task-related usefulness shaped viewing. Respondents described language learning and cultural learning as reasons to watch. The study recruited existing VTuber viewers from four Reddit communities, used convenience sampling, and did not collect nationality, so it explains motivations within a fan sample rather than prevalence among U.S. young adults. [S10]

The same study found that 37.9% of its respondents said they did not interact while watching. A live character should therefore serve active chat participants and quiet viewers. [S10]

### MWA implication

Blue’s defensible role is **educator, navigator, and host**:

- **Educator:** explain one researched concept, show sources, distinguish evidence from interpretation, and give the learner a bounded next step.
- **Navigator:** answer “where do I do this?” by opening the relevant course, guide, tool, quest, or settings path on screen.
- **Host:** acknowledge useful questions, summarize the room, connect related member contributions, and keep the schedule legible.
- **Companion with boundaries:** maintain warmth and continuity without claiming consciousness, therapy, friendship, or personal certainty.

The avatar creates an invitation. Blue’s task competence, consistent personality, and responsive teaching create repeat value. This interpretation follows the VTuber study and should be tested against MWA behavior data. [S10]

## 6. Online learning demand favors flexibility and self-direction, with weak generalizability

A 2025 Champlain College Online survey of 2,032 U.S. adults ages 18–24 who were attending or considering college found schedule flexibility selected by 73%, affordability by 65%, and self-paced learning by 63% as encouraging factors for online study. The publisher is an online college, and the public report does not disclose recruitment, weighting, questionnaire wording in full, or field dates. It is relevant to college-oriented young adults and should be treated as directional evidence. [S11]

An academic survey of 1,034 undergraduates across 2019–2023 found that modality preferences moved from a clear preference for in-person classes toward a more even distribution. Respondents associated in-person learning with support and human interaction, and online learning with flexibility and self-regulation. The sample came from undergraduates in unspecified classes rather than a representative U.S. panel. [S12]

### MWA implication

- Emphasize **short, self-paced missions** and clear resumability.
- Use the live educator to supply what asynchronous libraries often lack: orientation, interaction, and a sense of scheduled presence.
- Publish paths for different contexts: “I have five minutes,” “I want a full lesson,” and “I want to listen while doing something else.”
- Avoid claiming that young adults broadly prefer online education. The evidence supports flexibility as a valued attribute among selected learner samples.

## 7. Mental-health information, trust, and privacy require visible boundaries

Pew found that 32% of U.S. adults ages 18–29 used AI chatbots for health information at least sometimes. Among adults under 30 who used social media for health information, 46% rated it highly convenient and 39% rated it easy to understand. No more than 10% of any age group rated social-media health information highly accurate. The probability-based survey covered 5,111 U.S. adults in October 2025. These findings cover health broadly, not mental health alone. [S13]

Pew also found that 47% of men and 57% of women ages 18–29 said they ever received health or wellness information from social-media influencers or podcasts. Among young influencer consumers, mental-health exposure was common, but the sharp gender differences across beauty and fitness topics show why “Gen Z” cannot be treated as one audience. The survey covered 5,023 U.S. adults in June 2025. [S14]

KFF’s nationally representative 2023 survey of more than 6,000 U.S. adults found that 31% of adults ages 18–29 reported feeling lonely always or often. Rates varied across racial and ethnic groups. This measures loneliness; it does not show that a virtual character treats loneliness. [S15]

Pew’s 2023 privacy survey found that 73% of U.S. adults felt they had little or no control over company-collected data, and 77% had little or no trust in social-media leaders to admit mistakes and take responsibility for data misuse. These are all-adult figures, not estimates for ages 18–25. The probability-based survey included 5,101 U.S. adults. [S16]

### MWA implication

- Make Blue’s evidence behavior part of the format: display the source, publication date, population, and a one-line limitation.
- Separate educational material from diagnosis, treatment, and crisis response. Use reviewed escalation language for high-risk prompts.
- State what Blue knows from the current interaction and what she does not know. Do not imply private knowledge of a listener.
- Keep anonymous-by-default participation visible in product demonstrations. Explain data use in short, plain-language notices at the moment it matters.
- Design for quiet participation. Listening, saving, and completing a private reflection can be valid engagement.

## 8. Recommended role in the 30-day calendar

These allocations are planning hypotheses derived from the evidence above, not survey findings:

| Calendar share | Format | Job |
|---:|---|---|
| 30% | Short vertical video | Discovery through one question, finding, or product action |
| 20% | Blue product walkthroughs | Teach members how to use a course, guide, quest, or tool |
| 15% | Research explainers and source cards | Establish academic credibility and save value |
| 15% | Blue Radio live segments and recaps | Build scheduled presence and habitual listening |
| 10% | Game missions and progress moments | Convert viewing into bounded participation |
| 10% | Member questions, responses, and work | Demonstrate community and close the feedback loop |

Each week should include:

1. one searchable YouTube explanation;
2. three to five short clips derived from live or long-form work;
3. one scheduled Blue Radio teaching session;
4. one direct product walkthrough;
5. one research card with source limitations;
6. one action members can complete inside MWA.

### Tests to run during the first 30 days

- **Short video versus stream promotion:** Compare a concept-first clip with a schedule-first clip on qualified site visits.
- **Avatar versus task utility:** Compare Blue commentary with Blue demonstrating a course or guide.
- **Live versus replay:** Compare live attendance, replay starts, and seven-day product actions.
- **Audio versus video:** Offer the same lesson in both modes and compare completion and return.
- **Citation treatment:** Compare visible source cards with citations placed only in the description.
- **Segment differences:** Read results by age bands available in analytics, platform, new/returning status, and self-selected learning goal. Do not infer demographic identity from behavior.

## What the research does not establish

- It does not establish a single preference shared by everyone ages 18–25.
- It does not prove that a 24-hour stream will attract an audience.
- It does not show that VTuber popularity transfers automatically to an educational AI host.
- It does not support mental-health treatment claims for Blue.
- It does not identify the best posting time, clip length, or content cadence for MWA. Those require first-party experiments.
