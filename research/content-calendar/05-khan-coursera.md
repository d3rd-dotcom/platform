# Khan Academy and Coursera: competitor memo

Accessed 2026-07-17. This memo prioritizes first-party product pages, help centers, annual reporting, investor reporting, and first-party research summaries. Competitor statements are facts when cited. Recommendations and comparisons are labeled as inference. Mental Wealth Academy evidence comes from the repository’s canonical editorial guide and current Blue Radio implementation.

## Executive read

**Fact.** Khan Academy wins trust through a simple public promise, free access, mastery-based progression, school distribution, and repeated efficacy reporting. It reported 189.6 million registered users and 104.9 million yearly active learners for school year 2024–25. [K1]

**Fact.** Coursera wins through institution-backed supply, career-facing credentials, a large searchable catalog, consumer subscriptions, and enterprise distribution. It reported 205 million registered learners and 1,729 paid enterprise customers at 2026 Q1; its definition states that registered learners are cumulative accounts and do not measure active engagement. [C1]

**Inference.** Mental Wealth Academy should borrow their proof discipline, clear progress language, and repeatable editorial franchises. Its defensible lane is smaller and more participatory: behavioral psychology and media literacy inside a social gameworld, with missions, quests, user-owned rewards, anonymous-by-default participation, and Blue appearing across the experience. [M1]

## What each competitor teaches

### Khan Academy

**Fact.** Khan turns pedagogy into visible mechanics. A skill moves through Familiar, Proficient, and Mastered states; proficiency contributes to course levels and weekly streaks. [K4][K6]

**Inference.** Mental Wealth Academy should define the behavior behind every status marker. “Completed a field note,” “revised after Blue’s review,” and “applied a bias check to a live example” are stronger than unexplained points.

**Fact.** Khan’s editorial system repeatedly serves a job-to-be-done: back-to-school updates, parent dashboards, homework help, teacher lesson hooks, free family onboarding, and research summaries. Product education and acquisition often share the same article. [K8][K9][K10]

**Inference.** Build recurring series around real Academy tasks:

- “Blue’s field note”: one concept, one example, one prompt.
- “Use the Academy”: one screen or workflow explained in under a minute.
- “Research receipt”: method, sample, finding, and limitation.
- “Mission debrief”: what someone did, what changed, and what they earned.

### Coursera

**Fact.** Coursera packages authority through more than 375 university and industry partners, multiple credential types, and career language. [C1][C2]

**Inference.** Mental Wealth Academy can build authority through named researchers, cited methods, transparent reviews, and completed artifacts. The proof source should appear in the content itself.

**Fact.** Coursera converts platform behavior into an annual editorial asset. Its 2025 Global Skills Report used data from more than 170 million learners across more than 100 countries and turned enrollment trends into news, partner material, and search-friendly analysis. [C9]

**Inference.** A small project can use the same pattern without inflated scale. A monthly “Academy signal” can report one honest aggregate, such as the most revisited misconception or the mission with the highest revision rate, once privacy thresholds and measurement are established.

**Fact.** Coursera’s free first-module preview includes assessments and Coach where available, letting prospects experience the course before purchase. [C7]

**Inference.** Each social post should open a complete micro-experience: a bias test, reflection prompt, short Blue explanation, or one-step mission. The call to action continues the same task inside the Academy.

## Blue and Blue Radio: defensible positioning

**Repository fact.** The canonical specification describes Blue as a companion who guides users, reviews submissions, retains context, handles reward flow, and appears in reviews, rewards, surveys, and story moments. [M1]

**Repository fact.** The current Blue Radio code synchronizes listeners to the same position in a looping audio manifest, rejoins the current broadcast position after interruption, presents Blue on an animated stage, and describes the program as live “day and night.” [M2] The current code supports a persistent synchronized broadcast experience. Evidence for a continuously generated live lesson is absent from the reviewed implementation.

**Inference.** Position Blue Radio as an educator and platform guide through observable programming:

1. Teach one Academy concept per segment.
2. Demonstrate the matching guide, course, or mission.
3. Name the evidence source and its limit.
4. Give the listener one action that works immediately.
5. Route that action into a saved field note or mission.

This makes Blue more useful than a manual because she can explain the next relevant action in context. Use that comparative claim only after the broadcast reliably points to current interface paths and the team can measure successful task completion.

## Claims ledger for the content calendar

| Safe with current evidence | Requires measurement or implementation evidence |
| --- | --- |
| Social-first gameworld for learning, research, and rewards. [M1] | “Blue teaches live around the clock.” |
| Core courses and guides are free. [M1] | “Blue improves learning outcomes.” |
| Blue appears as a guide, reviewer, rewarder, and record keeper in the product specification. [M1] | “Rewards increase course completion.” |
| Blue Radio is a synchronized day-and-night broadcast loop in the current implementation. [M2] | “The stream is continuously generated or personalized.” |
| Participation is anonymous by default and rewards are designed to belong to the member. [M1] | “The Academy is more effective than Khan Academy or Coursera.” |

## Recommended content-calendar use

**Inference.** Use Khan’s rhythm for utility and Coursera’s rhythm for authority:

- Two practical tutorials each week: show a real platform task.
- One concept lesson each week: cite the research and include a prompt.
- One proof post each week: product receipt, aggregate, method note, or correction.
- One Blue Radio cut each week: a self-contained teaching segment that routes into the product.
- One community artifact each week: anonymized field note, revision, or quest result with permission.

Avoid competitor-sized claims. The strongest early content will make the product understandable, reveal how evidence is handled, and let people complete a small piece of the Academy before joining.
