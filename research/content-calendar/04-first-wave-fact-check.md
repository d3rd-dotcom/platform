# First-wave independent fact check

**Scope:** `01-yc-marketing.md`, `02-blockchain-marketing.md`, and `03-landing-page-audit.md`  
**Access date for web sources:** 2026-07-17  
**Method:** High-impact claims were checked against the cited first-party page, current regulatory guidance, the indexed production page, and the local repository. “Verified” means the cited source fits the claim. “Qualified” means the core is supported but attribution, scope, implementation, or inference must remain visible. “Unsupported” means the claim cannot be published from the evidence reviewed.

## Verdict

The YC memo is reliable and clearly labels its MWA adaptations as inference. The blockchain memo accurately reports the selected campaign figures and already distinguishes project-reported numbers from independent evidence. The landing-page audit correctly identifies substantial production-versus-local drift and proof gaps. Its Blue Radio, guide recommendation, quest-review, redlining, pricing, and free-course descriptions required tighter implementation language; those corrections were applied directly to `03-landing-page-audit.md`.

The central publication constraint is unchanged: the calendar may demonstrate Blue Radio as a continuous, VTuber-style platform-orientation broadcast. The current product does not substantiate a live generative AI educator, a 24-hour live stream, unique round-the-clock programming, real-time teaching, or superiority to a manual.

## Campaign metrics and regulatory publication rules

- Campaign counts must state the reporting party, unit, window, and as-of date. Project-reported participation is evidence of reported scale, not proof of retention, educational value, or causal impact.
- The MWA calendar’s percentages, proposed 10–20 invitations, five observed sessions, 25-note review cap, and 30-day cadence are planning choices. They must never be attributed to YC, a blockchain case study, or a regulator.
- Keep public copy free of chain names, tickers, wallet, token, mint, NFT, and similar infrastructure terms under `EDITORIAL.md`. The blockchain memo is internal research; its outward translations are the publishable layer.
- Use a deadline or scarcity claim only when the exact operational constraint exists. Record the capacity owner and contingency before publishing.
- Creator compensation, gifted access, or any other material connection must be disclosed with the endorsement. Repeat a disclosure periodically during a genuinely live stream.
- The FCA citation is a UK financial-promotion source. The FTC sources are U.S. staff guidance. Neither substitutes for jurisdiction-specific legal review.
