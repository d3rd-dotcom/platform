# Voice & Tone

Source of truth: `EDITORIAL.md` (Brand Book v4.0). This is the working filter.

## We sound like

- **Upbeat, academic, short, sweet** — the four words. Bright energy, real substance, no filler.
- **Internet savvy, never trend-hopping** — fluent in how people actually talk online, but we don't chase trends or lingo that will expire.
- **Debunking conspiracies online** — the energy of a good myth-buster: curious, evidence-first, patiently taking apart bad thinking.
- **Endless positivity and encouragement** — we root for the reader. The tone lifts. But the lift comes from concrete wins, not hype adjectives.

## We do NOT sound like

| Anti-pattern | Example | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate wellness | "Optimize your life!" | Name the actual mechanism or the concrete win |
| Vague self-help | "Step into your best era" | Replace with the specific behavioral frame |
| A recruiter | "Invite your friends and earn!" | Cut referral-bait entirely; talk about the work, not the funnel |

## The test before posting (run it first)

> **Does this communicate an educational gameworld, an academic aspect, or a feature — meaningfully?**

If it doesn't land on one of those three concretely, cut it or ground it.

## What we are (30-second version — memorize)

> **Mental Wealth Academy** is a cohort of scientists — survivors of trauma. We present case-studies on parasocial collaborations and HTCI (Human-To-Computer-Interaction) by funding decentralized game mechanisms on shared community infrastructure. The community benefits from Blue and from the enrichment of the decentralized network.

- **The Product:** a gameworld that is co-op, interactive, anonymous by default, and intellectually refreshing.
- **The Innovation:** we blend AI, blockchain, and science with gaming — which hasn't been done before.
- **The Difference:** a social-first gameworld with micro-learning through courses, missions, and quests that pay real USDC. We don't tack blockchain onto old workflows and call it innovation. Users never have to feel like a subject or a customer.

## Who we serve (pick the reader before you write)

| Audience | Who | What they want |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | Individuals seeking structured growth, **age 21–28** | Drawn to psychology and self-improvement; want accountability and real stakes; want a communal, social tribe |
| **Secondary** | Scientists and academics | Collaborative testing; research tied to live participants and real behavioral data; an AI companion generating novel datasets |

Most consumer surfaces (homepage, app onboarding) speak to the **Primary** reader. Don't mix the two audiences inside one section.

## Positioning (internal — keeps us honest)

**We are:** a research cohort for personal development, using agentic smart contracts. A research organization, experimenting with technology, ethically aligned toward making quality education easier.

**We are NOT:** a crypto education platform · a mental health app with tokens · an AI agent platform.

> The "we are NOT" list is **internal**. In outward copy, never write "this is X, not Y" (see `anti-patterns.md`). State what we are and let it stand.

## North Star

**Governance and self-repair.** Governance is how groups make decisions together — Blue's autonomous judgment is governance in practice. Quest completion is self-repair. Every part of the Academy practices what it teaches. When a line feels off, check whether it's pulling away from one of these poles.
