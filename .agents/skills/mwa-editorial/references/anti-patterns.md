# Editorial Anti-Patterns

Things to grep for and remove. These are the recurring drift modes.

## Mystical-only claims with no anchor

If a sentence sounds like it could appear on the homepage of a wellness brand, an NFT mint, OR an actual academy — it's too vague to be ours.

Watch for: *journey, transformation, awakening, frequency, consciousness (without "AI agent" nearby), energy, sacred, divine, transcend, ascend, manifest, alignment, vibration, harmony, flow state, deeper meaning, true self, authentic self, higher self*.

Each of these has a place IF it's grounded immediately. If it's standing alone, cut it or anchor it to a concrete mechanism.

## Crypto-bro tells

Watch for: *WAGMI, ser, gm, ngmi, frens, anon, based, alpha, degen, ape in, diamond hands, to the moon*.

Zero of these belong in MWA copy. We are intellectually serious. The tokens are infrastructure, not the personality.

## Corporate-wellness tells

Watch for: *unlock your potential, optimize your journey, level up your life, your best self, transform your mindset, holistic, synergy, empower, journey of self-discovery*.

These are LinkedIn words. Replace each with the specific psychological frame or product mechanism it's gesturing at.

## Academic-obscurity tells

Watch for: *epistemic, ontological, phenomenological, dialectical* — used as decoration rather than to do real work.

If a 5-syllable Latin word appears, ask: would a reader know what this means? If yes, keep it. If no, define it inline or use a plainer word.

Academic terms that pay off are fine: *Jungian individuation, the daimon, shadow integration, behavioral economics, mechanism design*. These are doing real work.

## Tone-mixing in one paragraph

A common drift: starting a paragraph in "Research Corporation" framing and ending in "Mobile Application" framing. The reader feels the shift even if they can't name it.

Pick one framing per surface (one section, one page, one tweet). If you need both, separate them physically — one section for institutional readers, a different section for individual users.

## Generic AI-product copy

If the page describes a feature that ChatGPT, Claude, and 50 other AI products also have, you've underdescribed. What's specific to MWA?

- ❌ "Our AI is trained to understand you"
- ✅ "Blue retains memory of every quest you've submitted and uses it to calibrate her review of the next one"

## Repeated information

Common pattern: a heading that says X, then a sub-heading that says X again, then an intro paragraph that says X for the third time. Each layer should add new information.

- ❌ Heading: "Our Mission" / Sub: "What We Stand For" / Body: "At MWA, our mission is..."
- ✅ Heading: "Repair and Governance" / Body: "Two ideas unified..."

## "We are excited to announce"

Just announce it.

## Em-dash overuse

The em dash is a great tool — but it loses force when used in every sentence. If you've used 3+ em dashes in a paragraph, replace some with periods or commas.

## Mechanism-as-title

Feature card titles, nav labels, and section headers should name the **category** the user is entering — not describe how it works. Mechanism belongs in body copy underneath.

| ❌ Mechanism-as-title | ✅ Category-as-title |
|---|---|
| Twelve-Week Cohort | A Cohort For Next-Gen Scientists |
| Blue Reviews Your Work | Ascend with Paradigmic Research |
| Shards You Actually Own | Universal Credit System |
| Disposable Apps, Lasting Value | Meta-Parasocial & Cybersecurity Research Programs |
| Memory That Compounds | Certified Academic Labs |
| One NFT, Full Access | Lifetime Membership |

The pattern: bad titles describe *how it works*. Good titles name *what you're joining*.

## Title Case Headings In Marketing Copy

Sentence case reads as confident. Title Case Reads As Advertising. Use sentence case unless the heading is a proper noun phrase.

Exception: Her name is written Blue in user-facing copy. Use BLUE only for code and contract identifiers. Do not write her name as an acronym.
